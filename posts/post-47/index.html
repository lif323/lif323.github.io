Optimal Ablation NeurIPS 2024

January 10, 2025

Optimal Ablation (OA) based methods have theoretical and empirical advantages over measuring importance via other ablation methods.

This paper states that ablation methods aim to answer the following question: What is the best performance on subtask $\mathcal{D}$ the model $M$ could have achieved without component $\mathcal{A}$ ?

$\mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X)$: the ablated model on component $\mathcal{A}$ is constructed by changing the value of $\mathcal{A}$, holding fixed all other parts of $\mathcal{M}$.

$\mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{A}}(x, a)$: computing $\mathcal{M}$ on input $x$ while setting the value of $\mathcal{A}$ to $a$ instead of $\mathcal{A}(x)$

Total ablation: A total ablation method satisfies $\mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X) = \mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{A}}(x, A)$ for some random $A$, where $A \perp X$.

I think a more direct statement of Total ablation $\mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X)$ as follows:

$$ \mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X) := \mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{A}}(x, A), A \perp X $$

For best performance, they give an example to illustrate it.

If two constructions of the ablated model $\mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X)$ both perform total ablation on Abut one performs worse than another, the former’s underperformance cannot be entirely be attributed to ablating A, since the latter also totally ablates Aand yet does not degrade performance to the same extent. Thus, the relevant $\mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X)$ for measuring importance should incur the minimum $\Delta$ among total ablation methods.

TWO Factors degrade the model performance after replacing $\mathcal{A}(x)$ with $a$.

Deletion: Replacing $\mathcal{A}(x)$ with some other value acould delete this information about $x$, hindering the model’s ability to compute the original $\mathcal{M}(x)$.

Spoofing: The replacement value acould “spoof” the downstream computation by inserting information about the input that either:

  • causes the model to treat x like a different input x′; or
  • causes the model to treat x in a way that it never treated any training input, if the new information is inconsistent with information about xderived from retained components.

Optimal Ablation: To ablate $\mathcal{A}$, we replace $\mathcal{A}(x)$ with an ‘optimal’ constant $a^{*}$.

$$ \mathcal{M}^{-\mathcal{A}}(X)_{(\text{opt})}(x):= \mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{A}}(x, a),\quad a^{*} = \argmin_{a}\mathbb{E}_{X\sim \mathcal{D}}\mathcal{L}(\mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{A}}(X, a), \mathcal{M}_{\mathcal{A}}(X)) $$

OA captures a maximal deletion effect because $A$ does not depend on $X$, and it minimizes the additional contribution of potential spoofing effects compared to other ablation methods.

References#

M. Li and L. Janson. “Optimal ablation for interpretability”. In: The Thirty-eighth Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Sys- tems. 2024.
