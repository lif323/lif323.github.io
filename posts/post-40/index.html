Note on What You See Is What You Classify

December 16, 2024

This paper examines existing works that primarily use activations and gradients or iteratively perturb the input to generate explanations. They train a second deep network, referred to as the Explainer, to predict attribution for a pre-trained black-box classifier, known as the Explanandum. In my opinion, the main contribution of this paper is the introduction of the area loss. This loss aims to constrain the number of non-zero attributions, $N$, to satisfy the formula $Za \leq N \leq Zb$, rather than merely minimizing the number of non-zero attributions, where $Z$ represents the total number of attributions.

$$ \begin{equation} \begin{aligned} \mathbf{q}_{\text{mask}} &= \operatorname{sort}(\operatorname{vector}(\mathbf{s})) \in [0, 1]^Z \\ \mathbf{q}_{\text{min}} &= [\mathbf{1}^{\lfloor Za\rfloor}, \cdots, \mathbf{0}^{\lfloor 1 - Za\rfloor}] \\ \mathbf{q}_{\text{max}} &= [\mathbf{1}^{\lfloor Zb \rfloor}, \cdots, \mathbf{0}^{\lfloor 1 - Zb \rfloor}] \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation} $$

Then, an area bounding measure, denoted as$\mathcal{B}$ is defined as follows:

$$ \mathcal{B}(\mathbf{s}) = \frac{1}{Z}\sum_{i=1}^{Z}\max(\mathbf{q}_{\text{min}}[i] - \mathbf{q}_{\text{mask}}[i], 0) + \frac{1}{Z}\sum_{i=1}^{Z}\max(\mathbf{q}_{\text{mask}}[i] - \mathbf{q}_{\text{max}}[i], 0). $$

where the first term forces the attribution $\mathbf{q}_{\text{mask}}[:\lfloor Za\rfloor]$ to approach 1, while the second term drives $\mathbf{q}_{\text{mask}}[\lfloor Z - Zb\rfloor:]$ to approach 0. Consequently, the number of non-zero attribution, N, satisfies $Za \leq N \leq Zb$.

References#

Stalder, Steven, et al. “What you see is what you classify: Black box attributions.” Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems 35 (2022): 84-94.
